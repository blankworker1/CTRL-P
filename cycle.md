# CYCLE


---

## CTRL–P Cycle Specification (v1)

A Cycle is the 30-day operational unit of CTRL–P.

It is defined entirely by the Slow Base-5 Clock.

A Cycle does not depend on human scheduling.

A Cycle begins and ends according to elapsed UTC time.


---

## Definition

One Cycle equals:

5 clock states

5 panel replacements

30 days (720 hours)

1 complete sequential rotation of all panels


A Cycle begins at state 0 and completes at the return to state 0.

Cycles repeat indefinitely.


---

## Structure

Each Cycle contains:

State 0 → Panel 1 replaced
State 1 → Panel 2 replaced
State 2 → Panel 3 replaced
State 3 → Panel 4 replaced
State 4 → Panel 5 replaced

After the fifth transition (Day 30), the next Cycle begins.

No overlap. No pause. No reset.


---

## Replacement Rule

During a Cycle:

Exactly one panel is replaced every 144 hours

Panels are replaced sequentially

Replaced panels are never edited

The act of replacement marks progression


Completion of five replacements constitutes one full Cycle.


---

## Output

A Cycle produces:

Five physical panels installed in sequence

A continuous hourly image record

A complete temporal rotation of the installation


The physical state at the end of a Cycle differs from the beginning.

The timing does not.


---

## Continuity

Cycles do not depend on:

Exhibition dates

Human intervention

Local calendars

Lunar alignment


Cycles depend only on the Base-5 Clock (v1).

If panels remain installed and transitions occur on time, the Cycle is valid.


---

## Integrity Conditions

A Cycle is valid if:

- All five replacements occur within their designated 144-hour windows

- No replacement is skipped

- No panel is modified after installation

- The feed remains sequential


If a replacement is missed, the Cycle continues, but the interruption is visible in the Feed.

The system does not compensate.


---

## Relationship to Nodes

Nodes execute Cycles.

The Clock defines when.

The Node performs the action.

The Feed records the progression.


---

## Principle

A Cycle is not an exhibition.

It is not a collection.

It is not a theme.

A Cycle is a complete rotation of time expressed through replacement.

Time establishes the boundary.

Replacement defines the movement.

Repetition sustains the protocol.
